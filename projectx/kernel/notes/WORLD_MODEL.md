# World Model

## General landscape
The field is real but under-aimed.
Existing institutions mostly optimize for smaller public endpoints such as medical restoration, human autonomy, product wedges, or generic AI capability.

## Strategic world facts
- Silicon is plausibly a viable substrate in principle.
- Continuity-preserving migration is not yet formalized.
- Bad methods failing continuity do not kill the target; they kill those methods.
- Existing industrial stack may be used without inheriting smaller missions.

## Hostile tendencies in the environment
- proxy substitution
- premature closure
- method lock-in
- institutional drift toward easier goals
- pressure to equate function-copy with transfer
